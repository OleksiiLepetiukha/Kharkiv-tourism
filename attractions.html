Kharkiv Famous Places

Mirror Stream (Ukrainian: Дзеркальний струмінь, Dzerkalʹnyi struminʹ) is a fountain in Kharkiv, Ukraine. The natural fountain was discovered in 1947.
The fountain is near the Kharkiv Philharmonic.It was built in 1947. It remains one of the most remarkable architectural monuments in Kharkiv and, due to that, has been listed in UNESCO's Encyclopedia.
Former "Komsomol Heroes Alley" as photographed in 2007 A "Komsomol Heroes Alley" with busts of young World War II heroes was located behind the fountain from 1958 until 2013. The busts were removed during the construction of the new church Khram Svyatykh Zhon-Myronosytsʹ, due to the 2015 decommunization laws they have never returned.

Sarzhin yar, obsolete. Sarzhyn Yar (Ukr. Sarzhyn Yar) is a gully (yar) over 12 km long with gentle slopes in the city of Kharkiv. Natural monument of local importance. It separates Pavlovo Pole (from the west) from Nagorny district (from the east). It is a recreation area. It is known as the source of mineral water. At the bottom flows the river Sarzhinka.
Sarzhin brook, the right tributary of the Sarzhinka River, flowed along the bottom of the pit from the former Otradniy farm. The brook was backfilled during the laying of the power line from Alekseevka to Pyatikhatki along the bottom of the pit in 2006 and at present, it is preserved in fragments. Not far from the Komsomolsky lake, a well near the forester's house is preserved in the pit (there used to be a natural monument, a 300-year-old oak, cut down for the purpose of installing a power transmission line near it).

Maxim Gorky Central Park for Culture and Recreation (Ukrainian: Парк культури і відпочинку «Максим Горький») is a Kharkiv, Ukraine, city park consisting of over 130 hectares of land. It is bounded in the southern corner by Vesnina Street in the east - Sumska Street to the north - the so-called elite private settlement construction, and in the west - Dynamo street corner and Novgorod.
The origin of the park was from the City governing council who "put forth a proposition to plant trees on 20-40,000 square metres on both sides of the Sumskoy road" The idea was that the area would be used for horse riding in a similar manner to the Boulogne Forest. Teachers, university students, and school students all assisted voluntarily with the tree planting. Each tree had a plaque planted next to it showing who had planted it.

Kharkiv Zoo (Ukr. Kharkiv Zoo) is a state zoological park in Kharkiv, the oldest in Ukraine. It was opened in 1895 and for visitors in 1903. It is located in the Nagorny district, in the Shevchenko Garden.
In August 2016, the zoo was closed for renovation. Initially it was planned to spend 104 million hryvnias on it and finish the work in 2018. As of September 2020, the total amount spent on the reconstruction of all zones of the zoo exceeded 2 billion hryvnias. In August 2021, two locations of the zoo out of 14 were opened to visitors, the reconstruction of other areas continues.

Shevchenko Garden is a city park in the center of Kharkov.
The garden was laid out in 1804-1805 by Vasiliy Nazarovich Karazin, the founder of Kharkov University. The garden was laid out in a natural oak grove on the outskirts of the city. On the upper terrace is a landscape park, on the lower - the botanical park. Since its inception, the garden was called the University Garden. In 1808 in the garden was built astronomical observatory. In 1896 in the western end of the park was opened the oldest in Ukraine, the third oldest in the Russian Empire Kharkov zoo.
In the 30s of the XX century there was conducted the reconstruction of the park. It was held by an architect V. Shirshov, a professor A. Kolesnikov and a dendrologist K. Kobezsky. New alleys were built, trees and shrubs were planted, and a fountain appeared. After the monument to Taras Shevchenko was opened in 1935, the garden was renamed after the poet.
